Find out which four drafts I liked least, starting with the utterly miserable and progressing to just sorta terrible.

For your reading pleasure, I’m proud to present the first installment of the 2013 fantasy draft report card. Read on to find out which four drafts I liked least, starting with the utterly miserable and progressing to the sort of terrible.

For reference you can find a spreadsheet of our league’s draft picks here.

Justin

Deez Nutz Reign On Your Parade

I’ll try and be as charitable as possible and just start with this: Justin’s fantasy football philosophy is very different from mine. I think he’s pretty happy with his draft, so good for him. But from my point of view, given what I said in the prelude post, his draft was a catastrophe of epic proportions. He started off by selecting Doug Martin over Adrian Peterson with the second overall pick. Now, paraphrasing what Justin told me yesterday, he feels that “results are the only thing that matters.” I couldn’t disagree more. Sure, it’s possible that Martin has a better season than Peterson. But is it likely? Was there a sound process behind the decision? I would have to argue no. There’s no “sure thing” in fantasy football but given AP’s track record he’s the closest there is. Further, Martin’s spectacular rookie season was powered by some long runs that are unlikely to repeat so Justin is definitely chasing an outlier here. Just for reference, 17% of Martin’s season rushing yards and 36% of his season rushing touchdowns came in a single game against the god-awful Raiders defense. If you take out that game and prorate his stats on a per-game basis he would have averaged 80 yards rushing and 0.46 rushing touchdowns per game. Last points: he only had five games last season where he ran for 100+ yards, and besides Arian Foster had the lowest YPC of any top-tier back. Moving on now. Besides Martin, Justin only drafted two other running backs, neither of which is a starter – this is that scarcity thing I was talking about. He also piled up his mid-round picks and set them on fire, with a defense in the fourth, a backup QB in the seventh and a fucking kicker in the eighth. Sure, the Niners defense is going to be great, but there were so many higher-value guys available, including Gio Bernard and Ahmad Bradshaw who came off with the next two picks. Hilariously, Justin drafted Bradshaw’s backup, Vick Ballard, one pick after that. The backup QB in the seventh, especially with a top-five guy like Cam already on the roster, is just silly. Finally, he went chasing an outlier again with kicker Blair Walsh. You already know my feelings on this but here’s a good read on that topic. Just piling on, but he also picked up a backup defense later on.

Note: Yes, I do realize that by writing all this I’ve all but guaranteed that Justin will somehow go undefeated.

Draft Grade: F

Jenny

Niner Faithful

I think this team is a disaster (no offense). Besides AJ Green and Peyton Manning I’m not excited about a single player on this squad. Jenny has basically one real running back, Eddie Lacy. We’ll see how Mendenhall does but my expectations are very low. Marques Colston is a stud but is also incredibly inconsistent. Greg Olsen is mediocre; you’d be hard pressed to find another tight end do so little with so many targets. The Seahawks D/ST will be good, and even if they allow more points and generate fewer takeaways than a year ago they’ll probably produce more touchdowns than the three they scored last season. Other things I dislike: drafting a kicker in the 11th, drafting Roy Helu at all, Santana Moss.

Note: I wrote this before the season opener. Peyton is currently on pace for 112 touchdowns this season – hey, maybe Jenny has a shot!

Another note: Dave pointed out to me that Jenny drafted AJ Green ahead of Calvin Johnson. Ditto my earlier point about Doug Martin / Adrian Peterson.

Draft Grade: D-

Ali

Chomo Se Dice: Salsa Con Queso

I’m not sure if Ali was even really trying. His team looks pretty awful and there isn’t really a plan here – he only drafted two receivers for Christ’s sake. For what it’s worth, I mentioned this to him on Thursday night and he seemed shocked. In the report card prelude I used Shonn Greene to exemplify the kind of low-ceiling player you should avoid late in drafts. Yeah, he’s on Ali’s team. I think DeAngelo Williams will be pretty good, as will Sproles and Gonzalez, and if everything goes his way it’s almost maybe conceivable that Ali’s team is decent. But I think it’s pretty unlikely that all of these work out:

  • Kaep has a colossal year, justifying the 18th overall pick.
  • Larry Fitzgerald is rejuvenated by Carson Palmer and returns to ‘07-‘08 form.
  • Arian Foster gets hurt, clearing the way for a monster season from Ben Tate.

I think this is going to be a rough season for Ali. Also, pick a new team name bro.

Draft Grade: D

ZM

Orange County Gunners

It’s not even worth writing up an auto-drafted roster. I don’t hate this lineup but contending for a championship with Ben Roethlisberger at the helm is nigh impossible in this league. And ZM: if I have to see “Orange County Gunners” on the scoreboard for another entire season there will be consequences.

Draft Grade: C-
